Statement of Risk & Regulations

Intermediate Category Eligibility Rules:

  1. We will conduct thorough due diligence on every participant.
  2. DUPR serves as an initial guideline but does not solely determine eligibility for the Intermediate category.
  3. Participants without a DUPR rating are welcome to participate in the Intermediate tournament, but due diligence will be conducted to assess their eligibility.
  4. Any participant found to have multiple accounts with DUPR ratings will be disqualified.
  5. Participants who have won any major tournament, whether on Reclub or elsewhere, will be reviewed to determine their eligibility to compete in the Intermediate category and will be moved to Open category if their skill level exceeds their current DUPR rating.
  6. If one participant is deemed eligible for the Open category while their partner remains in the Intermediate category, the Intermediate participant must find a replacement. Otherwise, the entire team will be moved to the Open category.
  7. Participants may report other participants they believe should be in the Open category. We will conduct due diligence, and the final decision will be made by committee.
  8. No refunds will be given if a participant is moved from the Intermediate to the Open category.
  9. If, during the tournament, we identify a team that was mistakenly placed in the Intermediate category but should be in the Open category, they may continue playing. However, if they finish in first place, their points will not be counted in the ranking system. They will still receive the prize and medals.

Tournament General Rules & Regulations:

  1. The tournament rules will be based on the USA Pickleball Official Rulebook. However, the Tournament Committee reserves the right to alter, amend or modify regulations and make the final decision on any dispute or other matters that may arise.
  2. Substitution or change in doubles partner will not be permitted after the draw has been finalised.
  3. All scores will be recorded and uploaded on DUPR.
  4. Teams arriving more than 10 minutes late or failing to show up for their matches will forfeit their matches, with the score recorded as 0-15.
  5. The tournament committee reserves the right to close registration once spots are filled, even if it's before the official closing date.
  6. The tournament committee reserves the right to modify, postpone, or cancel the tournament due to unforeseen circumstances. In such cases, registration fees will be refunded.

Registration:

  1. All participants must complete the registration process, including payment, with a partner before the registration deadline. Late registrations or incomplete entries will not be accepted.
  2. Participants are allowed to join multiple categories provided there are no scheduling conflicts.
  3. Registration fees are strictly non-refundable unless the event is cancelled by the organizer.
  4. Should the tournament be cancelled for any unforeseen circumstances, the registration fees will be refunded to participants, after deduction of administrative charges of RM 10.00.
  5. No refunds will be provided in case of cancellation by the participant.
  6. Prize values are subject to change if the registration falls below 50% of the intended capacity for a category.
  7. The organizer reserves the right to reassign players to appropriate categories if their skill levels are found to exceed the category's threshold.
  8. Participants are responsible for their travel and accommodation arrangements; we will not be held liable for any costs incurred.
  9. Each participant carefully verifies the supplied information and ensures its correctness.

Check In:

  1. All participants are required to register at the registration desk on the day of the event, at least 1 hour before the scheduled event.
  2. Identification document (i.e. NRIC or Passport) must be presented to the organizing team members upon check-in for verification purposes.

Code of Conduct:

  1. All participants are expected to demonstrate good sportsmanship and abide by the tournament rules. Misconduct, unsportsmanlike behavior may result in disqualification.

Health and Safety:

  1. The organizer will ensure a safe and well-organised environment, participants are expected to be responsible for their own health and fitness to compete in the tournament.
  2. By participating, participants acknowledge and accept the risks associated with pickleball and agree not to hold Courtsite or its organising committee liable for any injuries sustained during the event.
  3. Participants will need to acknowledge to the waiver and liabilities during registration as stated in Liability Waiver & Informed Consent.

Dress Code, Personal Belongings and Media Release:

  1. Participants must wear proper athletic attire and non marking court shoes.
  2. The organizers reserve the right to use photographs, videos, and other media from the event for non-commercial purposes.
  3. Participants are advised to bring minimal valuables to the venue.
  4. It is the participant's responsibility to keep their own belongings safe and secure while at the tournament venue. The organizer will not be held liable or responsible for any loss of personal belongings.

Dispute Resolution:

  1. Any disputes must be raised immediately with the referee or tournament director during the match.
  2. Only valid disputes regarding score discrepancies or rule violations will be considered, and no retrospective challenges will be entertained.
  3. In the event of a dispute, the Tournament Director's decision will be final.
